Instruction

Audio clips have been pre-labelled with a possible interruption where the first speaker may be stuck. Your task is to verify the candidate and annotate the interruption type.

Specifically, an interruption may happen when the original speaker gets stuck, and the other speaker helps by offering a word/phrase or asking a guiding question.

  1. Check whether the original speaker is stuck.
  2. If the speaker is stuck, choose the interruption type.
  3. Drag the bold red line on the audio waveform to the timestamp of the last stuck word before the other speaker steps in.
  4. Correct the interrupted or interrupting transcript if they are wrong.
Examples

Listen to these examples before beginning the annotation task.

Word/phrase (confident)

Speaker: ... show you the cheapest ...

Interrupter: place to get it

Word/phrase (guess/unsure)

Speaker: What is that show? The one with the ...

Interrupter: Twilight?

Guiding question

Speaker: It was like she turned this into like ...

Interrupter: What does it sound like?
