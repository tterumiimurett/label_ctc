Task

Instruction

We are looking for interruptions during a conversation and the type of interruption.

1. Collaborative Turn Completion (CTC)

Both speakers collaboratively complete a conversation for a natural interaction. There are several cases:

  1. The speaker got stuck. The interrupter provides a word/phrase in an assertive way to complete the sentence.
  2. The speaker got stuck. The interrupter guesses a word/phrase in an unsure way to complete the sentence.
  3. The speaker got stuck. The interrupter, instead of trying to complete the sentence, asks a question to guide the speaker.
  4. The speaker is not stuck. The interrupter buzzes in and speaks simultaneously with the speaker.

2. Pragmatic Pairs

This is not strictly an interruption, but requires the other speaker to intervene and provide a short response for a smoother conversation.

Audio examples can be found below.

Your task

  1. Identify what type is present: CTC, Pragmatic Pair, non-existent, or other.
  2. For CTC, select the specific type of CTC as well.
  3. Select the segment in the audio for the interrupted and interrupting utterances. The audio has been pre-segmented and transcribed to make your life easier, but there can be mistakes.
  4. You can edit, drag, and create segments on the audio waveform. Please also help us complete the transcript if you find any mistakes.
  5. When the speaker is stuck and the interrupter provides or guesses a word/phrase to complete the sentence, mark whether the interrupter's guess is correct.
  6. If after the interruption, the interrupter takes over the conversation and becomes the main speaker, please mark this.
  7. If you find more than one interruption in the audio, please label them.
Examples

Listen to these examples before beginning the annotation task.

CTC: Word/phrase (confident)

Speaker: ... like you know all these types of stores, and show you the cheapest ...

Interrupter: place to get it

CTC: Word/phrase (guess/unsure)

Speaker: What is that show? The one with the ... one with the wolf and one with the ...

Interrupter: Twilight?

CTC: Guiding question

Speaker: It was like she turned this into like ... Elf? Like in a m...m... But it was like ...

Interrupter: What does it sound like?

CTC: Buzz-in

Speaker: Taking a nap, making a breath, do something, whatever you do ... it's a quiet time.

Interrupter: Quiet time

Pragmatic Pair

Speaker: ... What do you call it?

Interrupter: Margin?

Later in the same clip:

Speaker: ... How do you say that?

Interrupter: Profit?
